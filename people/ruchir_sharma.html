Ruchir Sharma

Ruchir Sharma shows how starting early and staying focused can shape a long, meaningful career. He chose economics very young and committed to it fully, without getting distracted. Instead of only relying on reports and data from a desk, he traveled widely across emerging markets to understand reality on the ground. He believes economic success never lasts forever, so one must always question popular stories and look for the next opportunity that others are missing.

Another key lesson from Sharma is that mindset matters more than raw intelligence. In investing and in life, staying calm, disciplined, and patient during ups and downs is critical. He balances many interests—finance, writing bestselling books, and competitive athletics—through curiosity and grit. He also treats failures as learning moments, reflecting deeply and improving continuously. His belief in competition, deregulation, and lifelong skill-building helped drive his long journey at Morgan Stanley and later his success as an entrepreneur and global thinker.
